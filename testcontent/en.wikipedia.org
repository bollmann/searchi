<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 104px;"><a href="/wiki/File:UK1197Barr1944.jpg" class="image" title="Nicky Barr in 1944"><img alt="Nicky Barr in 1944" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/UK1197Barr1944.jpg/104px-UK1197Barr1944.jpg" width="104" height="139" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/UK1197Barr1944.jpg/156px-UK1197Barr1944.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/UK1197Barr1944.jpg/208px-UK1197Barr1944.jpg 2x" data-file-width="312" data-file-height="416" /></a></div>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Nicky_Barr" title="Nicky Barr">Nicky Barr</a></b> (1915–2006) was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_national_rugby_union_team" title="Australian national rugby union team" class="mw-redirect">Australian national rugby union team</a> who became a <a href="/wiki/Flying_ace" title="Flying ace">fighter ace</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Air_Force" title="Royal Australian Air Force">Royal Australian Air Force</a> (RAAF) during World War&#160;II. Selected to play for Australia on a tour of the United Kingdom in 1939, he had just arrived in England when war broke out and the tour was cancelled. He joined the RAAF in 1940 and was posted to <a href="/wiki/North_African_Campaign" title="North African Campaign">North Africa</a> with <a href="/wiki/No._3_Squadron_RAAF" title="No. 3 Squadron RAAF">No.&#160;3 Squadron</a> in September 1941. Barr's achievements as a combat pilot earned him the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Flying_Cross_(United_Kingdom)" title="Distinguished Flying Cross (United Kingdom)">Distinguished Flying Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medal_bar" title="Medal bar">Bar</a>. Shortly after taking command of No.&#160;3 Squadron in May 1942, he was shot down and captured by <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis forces</a>, and incarcerated in Italy. He escaped and assisted other <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> fugitives to safety, receiving for his efforts the <a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">Military Cross</a>, a rare honour for an RAAF pilot. Repatriated to England in 1944, he saw action during the <a href="/wiki/Operation_Overlord" title="Operation Overlord">invasion of Normandy</a> before returning to Australia as chief instructor with <a href="/wiki/No._2_Operational_Conversion_Unit_RAAF" title="No. 2 Operational Conversion Unit RAAF">No.&#160;2 Operational Training Unit</a>. He rejoined the RAAF as an active reserve officer from 1951 to 1953. After his subsequent success for many years in the <a href="/wiki/Oilseed" title="Oilseed" class="mw-redirect">oilseed</a> industry, he was appointed an <a href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Officer of the Order of the British Empire" class="mw-redirect">Officer of the Order of the British Empire</a> in 1983. (<a href="/wiki/Nicky_Barr" title="Nicky Barr"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alfonsin88.jpg" class="image" title="Raúl Alfonsín"><img alt="Raúl Alfonsín" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Alfonsin88.jpg/100px-Alfonsin88.jpg" width="100" height="130" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Alfonsin88.jpg/150px-Alfonsin88.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Alfonsin88.jpg/200px-Alfonsin88.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1353" data-file-height="1757" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Raúl Alfonsín</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1508" title="1508">1508</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a>, France, <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> formed the <b><a href="/wiki/War_of_the_League_of_Cambrai" title="War of the League of Cambrai">League of Cambrai</a></b>, an alliance against the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1884" title="1884">1884</a> – <i><b><a href="/wiki/Adventures_of_Huckleberry_Finn" title="Adventures of Huckleberry Finn">Adventures of Huckleberry Finn</a></b></i> by American author <a href="/wiki/Mark_Twain" title="Mark Twain">Mark Twain</a> was first published in the United Kingdom and Canada.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1907" title="1907">1907</a> – During the <b><a href="/wiki/Brown_Dog_affair" title="Brown Dog affair">Brown Dog affair</a></b>, about 1,000 protesters marched through <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and then clashed with 400 police officers in <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar_Square" title="Trafalgar Square">Trafalgar Square</a> over the existence of a memorial for animals which had been <a href="/wiki/Vivisection" title="Vivisection">vivisected</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (KMT) dictatorship of Taiwan <b><a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_Incident" title="Kaohsiung Incident">arrested</a></b> a large number of opposition leaders who had organized pro-democracy demonstrations, an incident credited with ending the KMT's rule in 2000.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1983" title="1983">1983</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Alfons%C3%ADn" title="Raúl Alfonsín">Raúl Alfonsín</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i> became the first democratically elected <a href="/wiki/President_of_Argentina" title="President of Argentina">President of Argentina</a> to take office after the fall of the <a href="/wiki/National_Reorganization_Process" title="National Reorganization Process">military dictatorship</a>.</li>
</ul>
